About me

From understanding people to designing for them

Neuroscience, communication and empathy: I bring to every interface the perspective of someone who studied how people process, feel and decide.

Origin

From Speech Therapist to UX/UI Designer

My first career was as a speech therapist and university preceptor. In trying to reinvent myself, I got lost — and in getting lost, I found UX/UI Design. I brought with me everything neuroscience taught me about how people process information, make decisions and connect with interfaces.

Beyond being a UX/UI Designer, I like to define myself as a cinephile who loves devouring sweets, discovering new cultures, enjoying good rock music, practicing Muay Thai, Jiu-Jitsu, and finding reasons to laugh in any situation.

2021 — receiving flowers from students

Fun fact

My roots

I'm from Rio Grande do Norte, a state in northeastern Brazil, and I carry my roots with pride: I'm a descendant of Portuguese settlers and the native peoples of my land. A fun fact that's part of my identity is the strength of the peoples of my region, who did not accept colonization passively and resisted with courage.

I'm very proud to carry in my blood the heritage of these strong people, marked by resistance, dignity, and a deep connection to their own history.

Goal

Professional Goal

I feel that the UX universe works like a big puzzle, where each project challenges me to piece together client needs and user satisfaction. My goal is to deliver useful, memorable, effective and pleasant experiences in every project. I enjoy translating complex ideas into visually attractive, user-centered interfaces.

My design is for and about people. In the end, it all comes down to creating something anyone can use easily and with satisfaction.
