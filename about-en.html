About me

First of all, who am I

Before talking about my professional journey, I want you to get to know a little more about who I am.

Origin

From Speech Therapist to UX/UI Designer

My first career was as a Speech Therapist and University Professor. Although I was achieving my goals, I felt I needed a fresh start.

In trying to find myself professionally, I got lost. And in getting lost, I found emotional places within me that I needed to explore. That's when I discovered the world of UX/UI Design.

2021 — receiving flowers from students

Beyond being a UX/UI Designer, I like to define myself as a cinephile who loves devouring sweets, discovering new cultures, enjoying good rock music, practicing Muay Thai, Jiu-Jitsu, and finding reasons to laugh in any situation. After all, I am nothing. I can be everything.

Professional

UX/UI is my calling

Over the past years, I've combined my knowledge in neuroscience, cognition, communication and behavior with UX/UI Design. I'm a UX Designer who loves creating clear and unique interfaces. I craft intuitive experiences for users in every project, blending creativity with functionality.

Results

Results and Impact

Over the past years, I've used my expertise to create over 25 distinct websites and systems for national and international clients. My portfolio includes collaborations with prominent institutions such as the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, Embrapa and Herbarium, as well as partnerships with renowned companies like Matinal, Hueb Ferreira Arquitetos and Akurey in Portugal.

"My design is for and about people. In the end, it all comes down to creating something anyone can use easily and with satisfaction."

Goal

Professional Goal

I feel that the UX universe works like a big puzzle, where each project challenges me to piece together client needs and user satisfaction. My goal is to deliver useful, memorable, effective and pleasant experiences in every project. I enjoy translating complex ideas into visually attractive, user-centered interfaces.
